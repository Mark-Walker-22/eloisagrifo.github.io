Eloísa Grifo



I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics at the University of Nebraska — Lincoln. My research is in commutative algebra. Before joining UNL, I was an Assistant Professor at the University of California, Riverside, a postdoc at The University of Michigan, and a graduate student at the University of Virginia, where my PhD advisor was Craig Huneke. I am originally from Portugal, where I first learned commutative algebra from Maria Vaz Pinto at Instituto Superior Técnico.

Keri Sather-Wagstaff and I organize the Commutative and Homological Algebra Market Presentations (CHAMP) seminar, a weekly online seminar showcasing the work of graduate students and early career researchers in Commutative Algebra who are on the job market. The seminar is currently on a break, and will restart in Fall 2021.
